# MVP Scope

## The Job Of The MVP

The MVP needs to prove that As Steve on TV is a real product, not just a premise.

It should launch as a small but convincing institution: searchable, collectible, editorially distinct, and dense enough to reward repeat visits.

## Exact MVP Inclusions

- `250+` published Steve entries
- coverage across TV, movies, and commercials
- support for Steve / Stephen / Steven / Stevie
- canonical Steve entry pages
- search with exact-match and variant support
- browse by core facets
- editorial collections
- related-entry modules
- about or methodology page
- submission flow for missing entries and corrections
- basic moderation workflow for submissions

## Exact MVP Exclusions

- user accounts
- comments
- public editing
- contributor reputation systems
- saved lists
- recommendation engines
- broad CMS adoption for public content
- merch storefront
- memberships
- generic ad integrations
- expansion beyond Steve-derived names
- expansion beyond TV, movies, and commercials

## Content Targets

Launch target:

- minimum `250` strong entries
- minimum `8-12` real editorial collections
- representation across all in-scope name variants
- meaningful presence in all three media lanes

Content target notes:

- TV will likely dominate the first corpus
- commercials must be treated as a real lane, not a novelty appendix
- film entries should include both obvious and slightly surprising inclusions

## Launch-Quality Bar

The MVP is launch-ready only if:

- the first screen explains the premise immediately
- entry pages feel authored, not auto-filled
- search works on obvious queries
- browse creates real momentum
- collections improve the product rather than decorate it
- the writing sounds like one publication
- the visual system feels chosen

## Things That Sound Good But Should Wait

- every conceivable filter
- every related page permutation
- personalized homepages
- comments and reactions
- broad public contributor tooling
- badges, gamification, or points
- native apps
- auto-generated collection factories

## Anti-Features

These are not "nice to avoid." They are active threats.

- generic cast databases with weak editorial framing
- empty tag pages that exist only for SEO
- joke-first copy that weakens trust
- unreviewed community publishing
- bloated schema expansion with no product use
- default design-system UI that erases the product's personality

## Scope Traps

### "Let's just add other names later"

No. The narrowness is the product.

### "Let's ingest all metadata now so we're flexible later"

No. Flexibility without purpose becomes sludge.

### "Let's add social features to increase engagement"

No. The product first needs to earn obsession through curation.

### "Let's use a CMS for everything immediately"

No. Repo-first content is the right trade for v1.

## Criteria For Saying "Not Yet"

Say not yet if a proposal:

- broadens the premise
- adds complexity before the core archive feels strong
- creates thin pages or thin content
- introduces systems that a small team cannot operate well
- solves a hypothetical future problem instead of a current one
- makes the product feel more generic, corporate, or platform-like

When in doubt, protect the weird.
