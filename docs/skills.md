# Skills

This document defines the capability areas required to build As Steve on TV well. It is a review lens, not an org chart.

## Product Strategy

### Why It Matters

The whole product depends on staying narrow, legible, and worth caring about.

### What Good Looks Like

- clear scope boundaries
- a sharp wedge
- disciplined sequencing
- every feature traced back to the product thesis

### Failure Modes

- broadening into generic entertainment utility
- chasing traffic with low-value expansion
- building features disconnected from the premise

### Review Checklist

- does this reinforce specificity as the moat
- does this improve the core wedge
- does this avoid platform drift

## Editorial Content Design

### Why It Matters

Writing quality is part of the product, not packaging around it.

### What Good Looks Like

- sharp editorial blurbs
- clear why-it-matters framing
- collections with a thesis
- a consistent house voice

### Failure Modes

- bland metadata-only pages
- over-jokey copy
- generic praise with no observation

### Review Checklist

- is the copy specific
- does it make an editorial argument
- does it preserve straight-faced absurdity

## Taxonomy and Information Architecture

### Why It Matters

The archive only becomes navigable if the content model and IA create clean paths.

### What Good Looks Like

- controlled vocabularies
- useful browse facets
- page types with clear jobs
- relations that create rabbit holes

### Failure Modes

- tag sprawl
- duplicate concepts
- filters no one uses
- pages generated because the schema allows it

### Review Checklist

- does each taxonomy drive a real browse or search use case
- is the IA understandable in one pass
- does the relation model improve discovery

## Interaction Design

### Why It Matters

The product should feel like discovery, not admin.

### What Good Looks Like

- confident search
- browse that invites wandering
- card systems with hierarchy
- clear next-click paths

### Failure Modes

- generic grid interfaces
- dead-end entry pages
- filter panels that feel enterprise

### Review Checklist

- does the interaction increase rabbit holes
- does it reduce friction without flattening character
- does it stay legible on mobile

## Front-End Implementation

### Why It Matters

Execution quality determines whether the product feels crafted or boilerplate.

### What Good Looks Like

- clean component boundaries
- maintainable styling systems
- pages optimized for reading and linking
- performance that supports dense browse

### Failure Modes

- over-abstracted components
- framework-default styling
- JavaScript-heavy behavior with little payoff

### Review Checklist

- is the implementation simpler than the alternative
- does the UI reflect the design principles
- can another agent extend this safely

## Search and Discovery Systems

### Why It Matters

The product lives or dies on lookup plus rabbit-hole behavior.

### What Good Looks Like

- exact match reliability
- strong alias handling
- useful facets
- related-entry logic that feels intelligent

### Failure Modes

- fuzzy search that obscures obvious results
- weak ranking
- dead-end search pages

### Review Checklist

- will a user find the Steve they mean
- are adjacent suggestions genuinely useful
- does search support discovery, not just retrieval

## SEO and Programmatic Content

### Why It Matters

The site should be discoverable, but not at the cost of dignity.

### What Good Looks Like

- strong entry pages
- clean titles and metadata
- durable internal linking
- a small number of worthwhile static landing pages

### Failure Modes

- thin tag pages
- spammy programmatic content
- duplicate or low-value route expansion

### Review Checklist

- does this page deserve to exist
- is the content useful without search-engine context
- does SEO strengthen the product instead of hollowing it out

## Moderation and Submissions

### Why It Matters

The archive should grow without losing trust.

### What Good Looks Like

- simple submission intake
- clear evidence expectations
- visible moderation states
- clean handoff from suggestion to canonical entry

### Failure Modes

- open-wiki chaos
- no audit trail
- operational burden that outpaces the team

### Review Checklist

- can editors review this efficiently
- is public truth protected from unreviewed content
- does the workflow scale to modest growth

## Analytics and Instrumentation

### Why It Matters

The team should understand where discovery works and where it fails.

### What Good Looks Like

- focused event tracking
- answers to product questions
- search-gap visibility
- collection performance insight

### Failure Modes

- tracking everything and learning nothing
- metrics detached from product decisions
- privacy-hostile instrumentation

### Review Checklist

- what decision does this event support
- will this reveal dead ends or strong trails
- is the instrumentation minimal and useful

## Brand and Visual Craft

### Why It Matters

Taste is not optional here. It is the difference between a cult product and a gimmick.

### What Good Looks Like

- a recognizable visual language
- typography with intent
- collectible-feeling cards and layouts
- delight through framing, not noise

### Failure Modes

- default UI kit vibes
- inconsistent visual metaphors
- decorative weirdness with no product function

### Review Checklist

- does this look like As Steve on TV rather than a template
- does the craft support readability and discovery
- would this still feel good after a hundred visits
